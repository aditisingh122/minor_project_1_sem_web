Women’s Rights

Understanding the journey, struggles, and achievements that secured women the fundamental rights they deserve — equality, safety, education, and dignity.

What Are Women’s Rights?

Women’s rights are the freedoms and entitlements of women and girls of all ages. These rights include the right to education, employment, equal pay, health care, property, voting, and freedom from violence or discrimination.

They are an integral part of universal human rights, established and protected through both global and national efforts over centuries.

Major Legal Acts for Women in India

  • The Hindu Succession Act (1956): Grants equal inheritance rights.
  • The Dowry Prohibition Act (1961): Prohibits giving or taking of dowry.
  • The Equal Remuneration Act (1976): Ensures equal pay for equal work.
  • The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act (2005): Provides legal protection against domestic abuse.
  • The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act (2013): Safeguards women’s dignity at work.
