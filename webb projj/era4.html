Global Empowerment (1990s–2000s)

As globalization connected the world, women broke national barriers — becoming CEOs, scientists, and world leaders. The decade was marked by the UN Beijing Declaration and global solidarity for equality.

Iconic Moments & Leaders

Global Milestones

UN Beijing Declaration (1995)

Adopted by 189 countries, it became the most progressive blueprint for advancing women’s rights worldwide.

Women in Leadership

From Indra Nooyi to Angela Merkel, women began leading Fortune 500 companies and nations — redefining global power.

Education & Health Access

Global programs expanded girls’ access to education and healthcare, reducing gender gaps across continents.

Science & Technology

Women entered STEM fields at unprecedented rates, driving innovation in computing, medicine, and space exploration.

“When women move forward, the world moves with them.” — UN Beijing Declaration, 1995
