Post-Independence Progress

From freedom to leadership — the decades following independence saw women redefining politics, education, and governance in India and across the world.

1947 — A New Constitution for Equality

The Constitution of India guaranteed women equal rights to vote, work, and own property — setting a legal foundation for gender equality in the world’s largest democracy.

1951 — First General Elections

Millions of Indian women cast their votes for the first time. It marked the beginning of women’s active participation in democratic governance.

1956 — Hindu Code Bills

The introduction of progressive laws on marriage, inheritance, and adoption gave women unprecedented social and legal freedoms.

1966 — India’s First Woman Prime Minister

Indira Gandhi became a symbol of leadership and strength, inspiring generations of women to pursue roles in politics and administration.
