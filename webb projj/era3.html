Legal & Workplace Rights

The 1970s–1980s marked a turning point — when women across the world challenged discrimination, demanded equal pay, and claimed their rightful place in workplaces and society.

Major Legal & Workplace Milestones

Equal Remuneration Act (1976)

Mandated equal pay for men and women performing the same or similar work — a landmark for labor equality.

Maternity Benefit Act (1961, amended 1980s)

Ensured job protection and paid leave for working mothers, paving the way for women’s health and workplace rights.

Rise of Women in Media & Education

Women journalists, professors, and administrators began breaking stereotypes and taking up leadership roles.

UN Decade for Women (1975–1985)

A global movement emphasizing gender equality, development, and peace — empowering activism worldwide.

Changing Workspaces

The 1970s brought a silent revolution — as offices, factories, and schools started to include women not as assistants but as professionals. This period saw women balancing home and work, often against social resistance.

Feminist movements spread awareness about workplace harassment and gender discrimination, which later inspired legal reforms like the Vishaka Guidelines in the 1990s.
